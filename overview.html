App Vetting

App vetting is a process aimed at determining if a mobile application (or app) conforms to an organization's security requirements and that the app is safe to deploy on the organization's mobile devices. An app vetting process is performed on an app after the app has been developed and released for distribution but prior to its deployment on an organization‘s mobile devices. Thus, an app vetting process is distinguished from software assurance processes that may occur during the software development life cycle of an app. Although an app vetting process typically involves analysis of an app‘s compiled, binary representation, it can also involve analysis of the app‘s source code if it is available. An app vetting process comprises a sequence of activities:
  • App Submission
  • App Testing
  • Report and Risk Auditing
  • App Approval/Rejection
  • App Deployment

App Submission

An app vetting process begins when an app is submitted by an Administrator or Mobile Device Manager (MDM) to an Analyst for processing. An Administrator or MDM is a member of an organization that is responsible for deploying, maintaining, and securing the organization‘s mobile devices as well as ensuring that deployed devices and their installed apps conform to the organization‘s security requirements. An Analyst is a member of an organization that is responsible for managing the app vetting process. Apps that are submitted by an Administrator or MDM for testing may be acquired from a number of third-party sources including app stores, web sites, or directly from developers. Note that the activities surrounding an Administrator‘s acquisition of an app are not included as part of an app vetting process.

App Testing

When an Analyst receives an app for processing, the Analyst will submit the app to one or more Analyzers for testing. An Analyzer is a standalone tool, online tool, or human that tests an app for certain characteristics with respect to efficiency, reliability, security, etc. After testing an app, an Analyzer will generates a report that identifies detected software characteristics. In addition, the analyzer will generate a risk score that estimates the likelihood that the detected characteristic will be exploited and the impact that the detected characteristic may have on the app or its related device or network. Risk scores are typically represented as ordinal values indicating the severity of the risk (e.g., low-, moderate-, or high-risk).

Report and Risk Auditing

After a report and risk score are generated by an Analyzer, they are made available to one or more Auditors of the organization. An Auditor is a member of the organization that inspects reports and risk scores from one or more Analyzers to ensure that an app meets the efficiency, reliability, and security requirements of the organization. An Auditor also evaluates additional criteria to determine if the app violates any organization-specific security requirements that cannot be ascertained by the Analyzers such as those that pertain to the use of the app (e.g., Classified apps must not be used by non-classified personnel).

App Approval/Rejection

After evaluating reports, risk scores, and additional criteria from all Analyzers, the Auditor(s) then collates this information into a single report and risk score and derives a recommendation for approving or rejecting the app for deployment based on the overall security (and efficiency/reliability) posture of the app. This recommendation is made available to an Approver who is a high-level member of the organization responsible for determining which apps may be deployed on the organization‘s mobile devices. An Approver uses the recommendations provided by one or more Auditors, as well as possibly other criteria, to determine the organization‘s official approval or rejection of an app.

App Deployment

If an app is approved by the Approver, the Administrator or MDM is then permitted to deploy the app on the organization's mobile devices. AppVet

AppVet is a web application for managing and automating the app vetting process, from the submission and testing of apps to the auditing of reports/risk scores and the approval/rejection of apps. Through the specification of APIs, schemas and requirements, AppVet is designed to easily and seamlessly integrate with a wide variety of third-party test tools including static and dynamic analyzers, anti-virus scanners, and vulnerability repositories as well as clients including app stores and continuous integration environments. In addition, AppVet provides a user interface (UI) that allows administrators, analysts, auditors, and approvers to manually upload apps, view reports and risk scores, provide recommendations and approve/reject apps. An AppVet system is a distributed system that comprises the AppVet web application and its associated third-party tools and clients. In an AppVet system, the app vetting workflow begins when a client or user submits an app to AppVet. When AppVet receives an app, it registers the app and automatically submits the app and related information to one or more Analyzers for testing. Unlike the general app vetting process where Analyzers may be standalone tools, online tools, or human analysts, AppVet requires Analyzers to be available as online tools in order to facilitate the automated testing of apps. For standalone tools, AppVet provides a service wrapper that can be used to convert a standalone tool into an online tool.

When an Analyzer completes its testing, it returns a report and risk score to AppVet. When AppVet receives reports and risk scores from all Analyzers, it then computes an overall risk score for the app and make this information, along with all reports and risk scores, available to one or more Auditors that, in turn, will use this information to derive a recommendation for approving or rejecting an app. This recommendation will then be used by an Approver to officially approve or reject the app for deployment on the organization's mobile devices.
